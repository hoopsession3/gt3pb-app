## Product System · Daypart Architecture

**Rise** — Cold brew + organic coconut (electrolytes). Morning activation.
**Flow** — Cold brew + organic cacao nibs + Ceylon cinnamon. Midday focus.
**Dusk** — Cold brew + Ceylon cinnamon + cardamom. Evening downshift.
**Performance Upgrade** — MCT oil or grass-fed butter, added to any tier.

**Brewing standard (locked):**
- 1:12 coffee-to-water ratio
- 16-hour cold extraction, 18°C, sealed lab-grade chamber (VIVOSUN)
- Single-origin organic 84+ SCA beans, within 14 days of roast
- Refractometer QC target: 1.30-1.50% TDS, 19-21% extraction yield
- Batch variance ≤0.05% TDS
- HOCl (Briotech) sanitation, glass-only vessels, ATP swab validation
- Rebrewed coffee ice (never water ice)

**Yield per 5-gallon water input at 1:12 ratio:**
- Concentrate served over coffee ice: ~50 finished 10oz bottles
- Standard 1:1 dilution: ~101 finished bottles

## Water Sourcing · Open Decision

**Primary candidate: Altamont Natural Spring Water** — Nantahala National Forest, NC. Sourced by Mountain Valley Spring Water of Asheville.
- Cost: ~$12.25-13.25 delivered per 5-gallon glass jug
- pH 7.2 (neutral, coffee-brew-friendly)
- Container: Glass at source
- Distance: ~65 miles from Greenville
- Regional Appalachian story alignment

**Mineral profile note:** Altamont is described as "low mineral content" — vendor has not published a full chemical analysis. Before final lock, request Ca / Mg / bicarbonate values direct from Mountain Valley Asheville to confirm cold brew extraction compatibility. If mineral content is under SCA cold brew ideal range (Ca <50 mg/L, Mg <10 mg/L), plan to remineralize with food-grade Ca / Mg salts.

**Fallback: Mountain Valley Spring Water** (Hot Springs, AR)
- Cost: ~$24.25 delivered per 5-gallon glass jug
- Known profile: TDS 220 ppm, Ca 78, Mg 6, HCO₃ 240, pH 7.7
- Established brand story (White House since 1924)
- Higher cost, less local, but chemistry is documented

**Decision framework:** If Altamont mineral profile confirms in workable range, use Altamont as house water. If not, use Altamont + targeted remineralization as the most coherent option (local water, dialed-in chemistry).

## Delivery Model · Phase 1 → Phase 2 Sequencing

### Phase 1: Sunday Direct Delivery (Months 1-3)

**Ordered and fulfilled through GT3 Performance Bar (app.gt3pb.com).**

**Mechanic:**
- **Delivery window:** Sunday, 5:00–8:00 AM ONLY
- **Order deadline:** Friday 6:00 PM (48-hour brew lead time)
- **Minimum order:** 12 bottles (any mix Rise / Flow / Dusk / Performance)
- **Delivery fee:** $10 flat, waived when order includes 24+ bottles
- **Coverage zone:** Greenville / Greer / Simpsonville / Mauldin / Travelers Rest (20-mi radius from GT3PB base)
- **Freshness window:** 7 days from delivery date
- **Payment:** On order (Square or Stripe), not COD

**Return bottle mechanic:**
- Customer specifies count of empties returning vs count of new bottles needed
- Customer sets rinsed empties on porch before 5 AM Sunday
- Delivery driver swaps: takes empties, drops fresh order
- Loop tier ($8) applies to returned bottles; New tier ($10) applies to fresh bottles
- Customer can mix: e.g., 8 refills + 4 new = 12-bottle order

**Delivery pricing at delivery (same as venue pricing):**
- Refills into returned bottles: $8/bottle
- New sealed bottles: $10/bottle
- Performance add-on: $14/bottle
- Delivery fee: $10 flat (waived on 24+ bottle orders)

**Sample 12-bottle order (8 refills, 4 new):**
- 8 × $8 = $64
- 4 × $10 = $40
- Delivery: $10
- **Total: $114**

**Why Sunday 5-8 AM only:**
1. Zero conflict with Saturday farmers market (brewed Saturday, delivered Sunday morning)
2. Compact 3-hour window, ~12 stops max, ~15 min per stop
3. Pre-dawn delivery hits the health-conscious customer's Sunday ritual
4. Weekly recurring rhythm builds habit
5. Freshness story is real: brewed <24 hours before delivery

### Phase 2: Third-Party Delivery Expansion (Months 4+)

**Partnership targets:** Uber Eats, DoorDash, Instacart
- Broader geographic reach beyond 20-mi radius
- 7-day delivery availability (not Sunday-only)
- Their driver networks, no direct labor
- ~30% platform commission

**Adjusted margins on third-party delivery:**
| Tier | Customer Pays | Platform Takes | You Net | Your Profit | Margin |
|------|--------------|----------------|---------|-------------|--------|
| Loop | $8 | $2.40 | $5.60 | $3.85 | 69% |
| New | $10 | $3.00 | $7.00 | $3.69 | 53% |
| Performance | $14 | $4.20 | $9.80 | $5.54 | 57% |

**Strategic split at Phase 2 maturity:**
- Sunday direct = loyal customer base, highest margin, weekly ritual
- Third-party = discovery channel, new customer acquisition, convenience

Direct customers become the Founding 100 — the base you own through the platform, not through the platform partner. Phase 1 must come first to establish this ownership before Phase 2 expansion.

## Banned Copy · Locked Rules

**These do not appear in any GT3 Brew or GT3 Performance Bar communication:**

- "Isn't X / it's Y" contrast flip structure
- Stacked adjectives ("clean, pure, natural, healthy")
- Wellness filler ("intentionality," "wellness journey," "mindful")
- "Detox" — prohibited entirely
- "Performance" as adjective in Brew hero copy (Bar language only)
- "Zenith" — stale blend name, replaced by Dusk
- FTC-flag language: "cleanest," curative claims, digestive claims, reflux claims
- "Meal replacement" — coffee is not a meal replacement in this system
- Raw milk anywhere in the recipe (removed for shelf life + health reasons)
- "Cold brew company" as brand identity (GT3 Brew is a beverage system, not a category)
